# Tracing Sycophancy

Large multi-modal models (LMMs) are pre-trained, then undergo RL. Throughout this process, sycophantic behavior emerges. Understanding when in the training process sycophantic behavior emerges is critical to mitigating it. Here, we present an evaluation of sycophantic behavior across base models and their post-trained counterparts. With three sycophancy datasets: factual sycophancy, opinion sycophancy, and implicit sycophany, we evaluate agreement rate, factual accuracy, hedging language, and refusal rate, to paint a clear picture of the types of sycophancy that LMMs exhibit.
